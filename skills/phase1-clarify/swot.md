# SWOT Analysis — Phase C Extension

Run this after `market-research.md` is complete. Feed its output directly in.

Two tracks: you pre-fill from everything you know, client reacts and adds. Never put a blank SWOT in front of a client and ask them to fill it out — you'll get aspirational nonsense. Give them something to push back on.

---

## Track 1: Pre-Fill from Research

Before the client conversation, populate what you already know:

**Strengths** — pull from:
- Problem statement (what makes their approach unique?)
- Buy vs Build assessment (any technical or domain advantage?)
- Client's own stated differentiators from Phase C

**Weaknesses** — pull from:
- Constraints captured in Phase C (budget, time, team size, tech debt)
- Gaps in the team or skillset
- Anything the client seemed uncertain or defensive about — those moments are data

**Opportunities** — pull from:
- Market Research: gaps nobody is closing
- Market Research: underserved audience
- Market Research: competitors' 3-star reviews (their pain = your opening)
- Industry trends and tailwinds (search: "[industry] trends [year]", "[industry] AI adoption", recent news)

**Threats** — pull from:
- Market Research: market leader's strengths and resources
- Market Research: well-funded challengers moving fast
- Global risks relevant to the industry (regulation, economic shifts, tech disruption)
- Switching costs — how easily can users leave once they're in?

---

## Track 2: Client Elicitation

Walk in with the SWOT pre-filled. Present it like this:

> "I've done my homework and filled most of this in already. Your job is to tell me where I'm wrong, and add whatever I missed. Let's go through it."

Then per quadrant:

**Strengths:**
> "I've put [X] and [Y] as your main strengths. What am I missing? What's the thing you have that would be genuinely hard for someone else to copy tomorrow?"

**Weaknesses:**
> "I flagged [constraint/gap] as a weakness. Be honest with me — what actually worries you about this? The stuff you wouldn't put in a pitch deck."

**Opportunities:**
> "Based on the market research, the biggest openings I see are [X and Y]. Anything else on your radar that I'm not seeing?"

**Threats:**
> "The things that could kill this or slow it down: [competitor strength, regulation, etc.]. What keeps you up at night that I haven't listed?"

Client corrections and additions get logged immediately. If they're defensive about a weakness — that's the real weakness. Note it.

---

## Research Moves (Independent)

Quick scan after client session to fill any gaps:

- **Industry trends:** "[industry] trends [year]", "[industry] AI disruption", "[industry] regulation changes"
- **Macro threats:** relevant economic or political risks in their target market
- **Competitor news:** "[competitor] funding", "[competitor] new feature", "[competitor] acquisition" — a well-funded competitor moving fast is a threat worth naming

---

## Exit Checklist
- [ ] Strengths: min 3, grounded in real evidence (wishful thinking doesn't count)
- [ ] Weaknesses: honest — if there's nothing uncomfortable in here, try harder
- [ ] Opportunities: tied to market research gaps and review mining insights
- [ ] Threats: at least one competitor-specific and one macro threat
- [ ] Client has reviewed, pushed back, and confirmed
- [ ] Output saved to `templates/swot.md`

Feed USP from market research + SWOT insights into positioning work in Phase S.
