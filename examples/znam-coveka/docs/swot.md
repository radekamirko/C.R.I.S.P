# SWOT Analysis

**Project:** Znam Coveka
**Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## Strengths

1. **No-bidding model** — the core mechanic removes the race-to-the-bottom that plagues every global competitor. Tradies pay for access, not per lead. This is a genuine structural differentiator.
2. **AI-generated match explanation** — no competitor (local or global) personalises the shortlist with a reason why each tradie fits the specific job. First in Serbia to do this.
3. **Bootstrapped = lean** — no investor pressure means the product can be tuned for quality over growth, which is the right call for a trust-based marketplace.
4. **First-mover in curated matching in Serbia** — Znam Majstora is a directory, Toolly is Uber-for-tradies. The curated-match niche is open.
5. **City-first density strategy** — Belgrade focus keeps supply/demand manageable at launch. Easier to achieve matching quality in one dense market than to spread thin across Serbia.
6. **Summer onboarding window** — tradies have downtime in summer, making them easier to recruit before the autumn busy season. Smart sequencing.

## Weaknesses

1. **Bootstrapped = limited runway** — no budget for paid acquisition on either side. Both tradie supply and homeowner demand must be seeded manually.
2. **Two-sided cold start problem** — tradies won't join without jobs; homeowners won't post without tradies. Chicken-and-egg, and manual seeding is slow.
3. **Solo founder building with Claude Code** — shipping speed is good, but capacity for parallel workstreams (product + sales + operations) is constrained.
4. **No brand recognition** — Znam Majstora has national TV coverage. Starting from zero in a market where trust is everything.
5. **Manual vetting at MVP** — quality signal is only as good as the vetting process, which is manual and doesn't scale without investment.

## Opportunities

1. **Broken lead-gen model globally** — Thumbtack and Bark are widely hated. Tradies actively seek alternatives. The pain is real and well-documented.
2. **No quality matching exists in Serbia** — local competitors are directories. The bar to be the best matching product in Serbia is low.
3. **AI as a genuine product feature** — unlike most platforms that bolt on AI as a marketing claim, the match explanation is a core UX differentiator that can be felt on the first use.
4. **Painters and plumbers = high frequency, high frustration** — both trades are in constant demand, are notoriously hard to find reliably, and homeowners have strong pain around bad experiences.
5. **Potential B2B expansion** — construction companies, property managers, and real estate agencies all need reliable tradies. Enterprise contracts could be a natural second phase.
6. **Regional expansion (ex-YU)** — Croatia, Bosnia, North Macedonia face the same problem. The model is portable once Belgrade proves traction.

## Threats

1. **Toolly could add AI matching** — they already have the supply side (insured contractors) and the booking infrastructure. If they add a matching layer, the gap closes fast.
2. **Znam Majstora funding / product upgrade** — they have brand and national recognition. If they invest in matching features, they have a distribution advantage.
3. **Global players entering Serbia** — Bark.com already operates in 10+ countries. A push into ex-YU markets is plausible as they expand in Eastern Europe.
4. **Tradie supply quality** — if the vetting is too light, bad experiences destroy trust early. One bad match in a small market travels by word of mouth.
5. **Low homeowner posting habit** — in Serbia, the default is still "ask a friend." Getting homeowners to change behaviour and post a job requires trust and marketing effort that costs money the platform doesn't have.
