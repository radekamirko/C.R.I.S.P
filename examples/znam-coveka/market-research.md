## Review Mining

### Thumbtack (PissedConsumer, Sitejabber, Reddit)
- "Lead costs increased to $30+ per lead, 9/10 times no response"
- "Fake leads — nonexistent names, disconnected phone numbers"
- "Same lead sent to 10 companies, Thumbtack makes $200 per fake lead"
- "Since [platform change] almost every lead is fake"
- "Charged $2,000 from my bank, refused refund"

**Signal:** Tradies desperately want a platform where leads are real and they are not competing on price.

### Bark.com (Sitejabber, PissedConsumer, G2)
- "When the lead actually responds, they say they never requested the service"
- "Extremely easy to use — but the leads are garbage"
- "High costs, poor quality, no refunds"
- 2.2/5 average rating from 1,644 reviews

**Signal:** Same pattern as Thumbtack. The lead-gen model is broken and tradies know it.

### Local (no formal review mining available — anecdotal from client)
- Homeowners rely on word of mouth because platforms don't provide enough trust signal
- Tradies waste time on platforms where homeowners browse but don't commit

## Positioning Gap

All existing platforms — local and global — are either:
1. **Directories:** homeowner does the work of choosing (browse, compare, call)
2. **Lead machines:** tradie pays per lead, competes on price, gets burned by fakes
3. **Instant booking:** Uber-style dispatch, no quality curation

**The gap:** A platform that curates 2-3 vetted matches per job with an AI-generated explanation of why each tradie fits — so the homeowner just picks, and the tradie never bids.

**USP:**
> "The only tradie platform in Serbia where homeowners get 3 AI-matched tradies with a personal reason why — no bidding, no fake leads, no scrolling through directories."
